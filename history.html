Let's simplify the history of cloud computing in an easy-to-understand form:
Imagine you have a computer at home, and you use it for everythingâ€”storing pictures, running programs, and more. Now, think about a time when people realized they could make a special kind of computer in a far-away place that everyone could share.

This special computer could store your pictures, run your programs, and you could access it from anywhere using the internet.This idea started to take shape in the early 2000s when a company called Amazon introduced a service called Amazon Web Services (AWS). It was like a big virtual warehouse of computers that people could use for various tasks. This was the beginning of what we now call cloud computing.

So, in simple terms, cloud computing is like having a magical computer in the sky that you and many others can use without having to worry about where it physically exists. This idea has grown over the years, and now, cloud computing is a fundamental part of how we store data, run applications, and connect to the digital world.
